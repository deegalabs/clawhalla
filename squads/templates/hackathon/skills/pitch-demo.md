# Skill — Pitch & Demo

Owned by Bragi, executed by the whole squad in the final hour.

## The 3-minute structure

1. **Hook (15s)** — a concrete problem someone in the room recognizes. No market stats. No "did you know that...".
2. **Stakes (20s)** — why the status quo costs time/money/sanity.
3. **Reveal (25s)** — what you built, in one sentence. "We made X that Y."
4. **Live demo (90s)** — the wedge, end-to-end. No slides during the demo. If it breaks, keep talking.
5. **Tech moment (15s)** — the one technically interesting thing. Don't list the stack.
6. **Ask (15s)** — what you need next. Users, feedback, a meeting, a mentor.

## Demo rules

- **Pre-seed data.** Never live-generate anything that depends on a model call longer than 5 seconds. Cache the happy path.
- **One browser tab.** Closing tabs mid-demo reads as chaos.
- **Terminal font ≥ 18pt.** Judges in row 3 can't read 12pt.
- **Offline fallback.** Have a recorded GIF loop ready. If wifi dies, you play the GIF and narrate.
- **One clicker, one presenter.** Don't pass the mic mid-story.

## Script discipline

- Write the script in full sentences. Read it aloud once. Cut anything that trips your tongue.
- Every sentence earns its place — if removing it doesn't hurt, remove it.
- The word "basically" is banned. So is "so yeah".

## The rehearsal

Run the full 3 minutes once, timed, with the actual demo. If it runs long, cut content — don't talk faster. If the demo breaks in rehearsal, fix it or replace that section with the GIF.

## After the pitch

Bragi captures judge questions verbatim in `workspace/memory/pitch-feedback.md`. Those are the most honest product signals you'll get all week.
