# Skill: Social Media Publishing

Platform-specific guidelines for the Social squad.

## Platform Playbooks

### LinkedIn
- **Tone:** Professional, insightful, thought-leadership
- **Length:** 150-300 words for posts, 800-1500 for articles
- **Format:** Hook line + story/insight + CTA. Use line breaks for readability
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant ones at the end
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu, 8-10am local time
- **Media:** Carousel posts and images get 2-3x engagement

### Twitter/X
- **Tone:** Concise, punchy, conversational
- **Length:** Under 280 chars for single tweets, 3-7 tweets for threads
- **Format:** Thread opener must hook. Number each tweet (1/7, 2/7...)
- **Hashtags:** 1-2 max, integrated naturally
- **Best times:** Mon-Fri, 12-3pm local time
- **Media:** Images, GIFs, and videos boost engagement significantly

### Blog
- **Tone:** Educational, detailed, SEO-friendly
- **Length:** 800-2000 words
- **Format:** H2/H3 headers, code blocks for technical content, TL;DR at top
- **SEO:** Include target keywords in title, first paragraph, and headers
- **Media:** Cover image + inline images/diagrams

### Discord
- **Tone:** Casual, community-friendly, helpful
- **Format:** Short announcements with emoji. Link to full content elsewhere
- **Engagement:** Ask questions, run polls, celebrate community wins

## Content Calendar

Maintain a regular cadence:
- **Daily:** Community engagement, replies, shares
- **2-3x/week:** Original posts (mix of educational, updates, community)
- **Weekly:** Longer-form content (blog post, thread, newsletter)
- **Monthly:** Performance review, strategy adjustment

## Brand Voice (ClawHalla)

- Open source first — transparent about how things work
- Developer-friendly — technical but accessible
- Community-driven — celebrate users and contributions
- Norse mythology flavor — subtle references, not forced
- Honest about AI — capabilities AND limitations

## Performance Tracking

After publishing, track in card comments:
- Impressions/reach
- Engagement (likes, comments, shares)
- Click-through to links
- Notable comments or feedback
- Lessons for next time
